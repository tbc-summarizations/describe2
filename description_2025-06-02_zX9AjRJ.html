Alright, here's my brutally honest take on Matchday 1 of the "best" European cup tournament group stages: • Spain vs. France (0-0): What a snoozefest. Spain, still trying to relive their glory days from a decade ago, play like they're allergic to scoring. France, with all their supposed talent, couldn't even break down a team that looks like they're stuck in slow motion. • Netherlands vs. England (1-1): A pathetic display from two overhyped teams. The Netherlands, consistently disappointing, barely scraped a draw. England, as always, failed to live up to the ridiculous expectations their fans have of them. 1-1 is the score, but both teams deserve a loss. • Portugal vs. Belgium (0-1): Portugal, still relying on an aging Ronaldo, got outplayed by Belgium. Belgium, while decent, didn't exactly set the world on fire either. They just managed to win by a single goal against a team that refuses to move on from its past successes. • Germany vs. Italy (2-2): A somewhat interesting game, but both teams showed their defensive frailties. Germany continues to be a shadow of its former self, while Italy still needs to prove they are good. 2-2 a pretty weak result. • Croatia vs. Switzerland (2-1): Croatia showing they can beat an average team. Switzerland, another team that consistently underperforms, almost managed to draw, but fell short. This match had the energy of a friendly game. • Austria vs. Denmark (1-2): Austria, as per usual, showed they are not a team to fear. Denmark, while slightly better, only managed a narrow victory. Who cares about this game? • Ukraine vs. Turkey (1-0): Ukraine with a lucky goal against Turkey. Turkey, yet another team that talks a big game but delivers nothing. This match will be forgotten by tomorrow. • Wales vs. Sweden (0-2): Wales got destroyed, with their performance showing why they're a fringe team in the tournament. Sweden, while not impressive, at least managed to secure a comfortable win. A boring result to end a boring matchday. Overall, Matchday 1 showed why European football is becoming increasingly stale. Overrated teams, predictable results, and a general lack of excitement. Bring on the next round, where hopefully, the teams will have something more to offer than mediocrity.
