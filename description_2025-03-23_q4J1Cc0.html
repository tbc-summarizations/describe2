Facts & Technical Stuff • Battery: Expect a large battery pack, the exact capacity varying based on route requirements and operator specs. Wrightbus claims decent range, but real-world performance can be affected by weather, route topography, and driving style. • Charging: These require charging infrastructure, either overnight depot charging or potentially opportunity charging at bus stops. Charging times can be significant, a real operational hurdle. • Weight: Electric buses are heavy due to batteries, which means greater wear and tear on roads and potentially reduced passenger capacity compared to diesel buses. • LV23 EZA: Registration Plate details of this bus. • ES 33: Fleet number of this bus.
