Alright, here's my brutally honest take on those countries and their natural beauty, ranked out of 10: • Malaysia 🇲🇾: 9/10. The rainforests are incredible, the beaches are stunning, and the biodiversity is off the charts. It's one of the most naturally beautiful countries in Southeast Asia. • Peru 🇵🇪: 9/10. The Andes Mountains, the Amazon rainforest, and the coastline... Peru has some of the most diverse and impressive landscapes on the planet. • Bosnia 🇧🇦: 8/10. Seriously underrated. The mountains are rugged, the rivers are crystal clear, and the forests are dense and unspoiled. • Greece 🇬🇷: 7/10. The coastlines and islands are beautiful, and the mountains are dramatic. • Kazakhstan 🇰🇿: 6/10. Vast and diverse landscapes, from deserts to mountains to steppes. • Serbia 🇷🇸: 6/10. Has potential with its mountains and forests, but it needs more investment in preserving its natural beauty. • Estonia 🇪🇪: 5/10. Flat and forested, which isn't a bad thing, but it lacks the dramatic landscapes of some of the other countries on the list. • Poland 🇵🇱: 5/10. Similar to Estonia, it has some nice forests and lakes, but nothing that really stands out. • England 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿: 4/10. The countryside can be quaint, but it's mostly just rolling hills and farmland. • Egypt 🇪🇬: 3/10. The Nile is impressive, but the rest of the country is mostly desert. Not much natural beauty to speak of.
