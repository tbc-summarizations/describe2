Alright, strap in for this wild ride. We're about to witness the chaotic collision of Kanye and The Beatles, a musical trainwreck that's somehow compelling. Album Title: Hey Yeezus Concept: A fusion of Yeezy's abrasive, experimental sound with The Beatles' timeless melodies and songwriting. Think gospel choirs battling distorted 808s. Tracklist: 1. "Revolution 909 (Slavery Was a Choice)" (4:15) • Features: Pusha T • Producers: Kanye West, Mike Dean • Writers: John Lennon, Paul McCartney, Kanye West, Pusha T • Description: The album opener. Starts with a distorted sample of "Revolution 9" before a jarring beat drop. Kanye raps about breaking free from societal norms and, of course, that slavery line that sets Twitter ablaze. Pusha T delivers a coke-laced verse about the corrupting power of money. 2. "All You Need is YEEZUS" (3:50) • Features: Kid Cudi • Producers: Kanye West, Jeff Bhasker • Writers: John Lennon, Paul McCartney, Kanye West, Scott Mescudi • Description: A reimagining of "All You Need Is Love" with a dark, gospel-infused twist. Auto-tuned vocals, heavy synths, and Kid Cudi humming in the background. 3. "Black Skinhead Submarine" (4:55) • Features: Rihanna • Producers: Kanye West, Rick Rubin • Writers: John Lennon, Paul McCartney, Kanye West • Description: A sinister blend of "Black Skinhead" and "Yellow Submarine." Rihanna's ethereal vocals are juxtaposed against Kanye's primal screams. A haunting, experimental track that explores themes of paranoia and isolation. 4. "Let It (B)leed" (5:20) • Features: Bon Iver • Producers: Kanye West, Justin Vernon • Writers: John Lennon, Paul McCartney, Kanye West, Justin Vernon • Description: A melancholy track built around a sample of "Let It Be." "I Am the Walrus, Bitch" (3:30) • Features: Nicki Minaj • Producers: Kanye West, No ID • Writers: John Lennon, Kanye West, Onika Tanya Maraj • Description: A straight-up banger. "Yesterday (I Thought About Killing You)" (4:00) • Features: None • Producers: Kanye West, Arca • Writers: Paul McCartney, Kanye West • Description: A stripped-down, confessional track where Kanye reflects on his past mistakes and suicidal thoughts.
