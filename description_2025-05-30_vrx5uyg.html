Alright, let's break down these Euro Quarter-Finals with no BS: Germany vs. England: Germany 3 - 1 England • Germany: Ruthless efficiency is their game. They'll exploit England's defensive frailties, especially down the flanks. Their midfield, even if not at its peak, is tactically superior. • England: Overhyped. Their attack lacks the killer instinct against top-tier opponents. Their defense will crumble under sustained German pressure. Expect them to be a disappointment again. Belgium vs. Portugal: Belgium 2 - 0 Portugal • Belgium: The Golden Generation, despite aging, still has bite. Their attack is too dynamic and versatile for Portugal's aging defense. • Portugal: Ronaldo's brilliance can only carry them so far. Their midfield is pedestrian, and their defense is prone to errors. A one-man team will fall short against a well-rounded Belgium. Switzerland vs. Netherlands: Switzerland 1 - 2 Netherlands • Switzerland: Determined and organized, but ultimately outclassed. They'll put up a fight, but lack the individual brilliance to match the Dutch. • Netherlands: Finally playing with some swagger. Their attack is explosive, and their midfield is starting to gel. They'll overcome Swiss resilience with sheer firepower. France vs. Spain: France 2 - 1 Spain • France: The team to beat. Mbappe, Griezmann, and Benzema – enough said. Their possession-based style will be stifled by France's physicality and tactical nous. They'll lack the cutting edge to break down the French defense.
